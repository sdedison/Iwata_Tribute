Satoru Iwata

Video games are meant to be one thing: Fun, fun for everyone

Saturo Iwata was president & CEO of Nintendo from 2002 until his unexpected passage in 2015. He was 55 years old.

Iwata's Life Timeline

  • 1959:  Born in Sapporo, Japan
  • 1975:  Attends Hokkaido Sapporo South High School and begins programming games on his calculator
  • 1982:  Graduates from the Tokyo Institute of Technology with a degree in Computer Science
  • 1983:  Joins HAL Laboratories as a programmer and quickly becomes director of software production
  • 1993:  Becomes president of HAL Laboratories and saves the company from bankruptcy
  • 2000:  Moves to Nintendo and becomes the head of corporate planning
  • 2002:  Is appointed CEO of Nintendo, the first CEO outside of the Yamauchi family
  • 2006:  Nintendo launches the popular Wii console, fulfilling Iwata's desire to open gaming up to a broader audience
  • 2007:  Wii Fit launces for the Wii, an initial offering in Iwata's "quality of life" initiative
  • 2012:  Nintendo launches the Wii's successor the Wii U, it ultimately flops
  • 2014:  Cuts his own salary by 50% following the Wii U's poor performance
  • 2015:  Nintendo's performance improves following the launch of Amiibo and mobile games, Iwata passes
  • 2017:  The Nintedo Switch launches, (the last console developed under Iwata's influence), to great initial success

"On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."

— Satoru Iwata
